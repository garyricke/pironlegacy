Behind the Build

How the Lead Donor Profiles Were Built

Each lead-donor profile pairs a studio-quality portrait with a short researched story, key facts, and links. Because most donors don’t have a polished headshot on hand, we use AI image tools to build one from the photos they already have — then host everything on Cloudinary. Here’s exactly how the first two were made, and what to collect from the next donors.

1 · Rob Hollis — a studio portrait synthesized from casual photos

Rob didn’t have a professional headshot, so we generated one from everyday photos he supplied.

Both were generated by editing from Rob’s real photos (not invented from scratch), so the likeness stays true. We picked the Gemini version for its faithful glasses/hairline, cleaner backdrop, and better handling of the original glare.

3 · What to request from the next lead donors

To build each profile at this quality, ask every donor for:
  • A professional headshot if they have one — otherwise 3–5 clear, well-lit photos (front-on, head & shoulders, a couple of angles). Higher resolution is better.
  • One candid “in their element” photo (at their business, on-site, or with family) for a warm moment inside the profile.
  • A few sentences on their connection to Dennis / the INCubator and why they’re supporting the scholarship.
  • Links to feature — LinkedIn, their company, or their organization.
  • Their OK to feature an AI-assisted portrait and published bio — likeness and copy approved before go-live.
